Vada Pav Recipe

Description

Vada Pav is a popular street food from Maharashtra, India. It consists of a spicy, mashed potato filling encased in a gram flour batter, deep-fried until crispy, and served inside a pav (bread roll). It is typically garnished with chutneys and fried green chilies. This savory snack is loved by people across India and is often enjoyed as a quick, filling meal.

Ingredients

For the Vada (Potato Filling):

  • 4 medium-sized boiled potatoes
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Oil (for frying)

For the Pav (Bread Rolls):

  • 6 pav buns (Indian bread rolls)
  • Green chutney (optional)
  • Tamarind chutney (optional)
  • Dry garlic chutney (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Vada Filling: Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes to the pan and mix everything until well combined. Cook for 2-3 minutes and remove from heat.
  4. Let the mixture cool down a bit, then shape it into round balls (vada).
  5. Make the Batter: In a bowl, add besan (gram flour), a pinch of turmeric, salt, and water to make a smooth batter (it should coat the vada balls). Heat oil in a pan for deep frying.
  6. Dip each vada ball in the batter and deep fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  7. Assemble the Vada Pav: Slit the pav buns horizontally. Spread green chutney and tamarind chutney on the inside if desired.
  8. Place one hot vada in each pav, optionally sprinkle some dry garlic chutney, and serve immediately with a side of fried green chilies.
